Hari Raya Aidilfitri

Hari Raya Aidilfitri, also known as Eid al-Fitr, is a significant Muslim holiday celebrated worldwide. It marks the end of Ramadan, a month of fasting and prayer. During this joyous occasion, Muslims don their finest clothes, clean and decorate their homes, and prepare special meals and sweets. The day begins with a communal prayer at the mosque, emphasizing unity and gratitude. Charity is a key aspect, with Muslims giving to those in need. Visiting family and friends, exchanging gifts, and sharing traditional dishes are customary. 'Selamat Hari Raya' or 'Eid Mubarak' greetings abound, fostering a sense of love, forgiveness, and community.

Hari Raya Puasa

Hari Raya Puasa, also known as Eid al-Fitr, is a significant religious festival celebrated by Muslims worldwide. It marks the conclusion of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting and spiritual reflection. During Hari Raya Puasa, Muslims dress in their finest attire, decorate their homes, and partake in festive meals. The day commences with a communal prayer at the mosque, fostering a sense of unity and gratitude. Charity is a central tenet, with Muslims offering donations to the less fortunate. Visiting friends and family, exchanging gifts, and sharing traditional dishes are common customs, while warm greetings like 'Selamat Hari Raya' or 'Eid Mubarak' are exchanged to spread love and goodwill.

Awal Muharram

Awal Muharram, also known as Islamic New Year or Hijri New Year, marks the beginning of the Islamic lunar calendar year. It's a significant occasion for Muslims, signifying a time of reflection, renewal, and spiritual growth. While it is not as elaborate as some other Islamic festivals, it's a time for Muslims to remember the migration (Hijra) of Prophet Muhammad from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE, which holds historical and religious importance. Muslims may engage in prayers, attend sermons, and make resolutions for self-improvement in the year ahead. The date of Awal Muharram varies each year since the Islamic calendar is lunar-based.
