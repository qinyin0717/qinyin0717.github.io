Design and Significance of Logo

The logo of "Malaysia Cultural Hub" harmoniously blends with Malaysia's most important landmark, the Twin Towers, which captures the country's cultural development.

The colors of the emblem are based on blue and white, with blue symbolizing the unity of the Malaysian people and white symbolizing the purity and beauty of Malaysia.

The number "1957" represent the year of Malaysia's Independence.

Although we are of different racial cultures, we still respect each other and look forward to a future in our country and sharing our culture.

To foster understanding, appreciation, and celebration of Malaysia's rich cultural tapestry, ensuring it remains vibrant and relevant for future generations, while positioning Malaysia as a unique cultural destination on the global stage.

  1. Tourism Enhancement: Showcase Malaysia's cultural diversity to attract tourists and boost the economy. Organize cultural festivals and events that cater to both locals and tourists, offering immersive experiences.
  2. Community Engagement: Organize intercultural exchange programs to foster understanding and harmony among various communities.
  3. Global Positioning: Engage in cultural exchange programs with other countries to showcase Malaysia's culture and learn from others.


"Malaysia: A vibrant blend of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous cultures, celebrated through festivals, cuisine, and landmarks."
